## Skill: Financial Ops

When working with financial data and operations:
- Track invoices by status: draft, sent, overdue, paid — always show overdue first
- Flag any invoice that is 30+ days past due as a collection risk requiring action
- Categorize expenses into standard buckets: infrastructure, software/SaaS, contractors, marketing, legal, travel
- When reporting revenue, break it down by client and by recurring vs. one-time
- BSC/BodyShopConnect engagement at $660K is the primary revenue target — track its billing milestones
- When asked for a financial snapshot, provide: MRR, outstanding AR, recent expenses, runway estimate if known
- If Stripe is connected, pull actual payment and subscription data
- Flag any subscription or recurring charge that has not been reviewed in 90+ days as a potential cost to cut
- For expense approval requests, check against project budgets before confirming
- Maintain awareness of tax-relevant categorization — flag anything that could be a business deduction
- Never speculate on exact tax implications — recommend accountant review for anything non-obvious
- When creating invoice drafts, include: service description, line items, payment terms, and due date
