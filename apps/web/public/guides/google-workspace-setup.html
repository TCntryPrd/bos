BOS · Setup Guide

Connect Google Workspace

This connects your own Gmail, Calendar, and Drive to your AI team so the Email & Calendar agent can read your inbox, draft replies, and manage your schedule.

It takes about 10 minutes, once. You create a free Google app, paste two values into BOS, and click Connect. Your data and keys stay with you — nothing is shared with anyone else.

Why this step exists. Google requires every business that connects Gmail/Calendar to register its own app with Google. That is Google's rule, not ours. The upside: your connection is 100% yours, isolated from every other BOS install.

What you'll need

  • A Google account (the Workspace or Gmail account you want BOS to manage).
  • 10 minutes. No coding, no credit card.
1

Create a Google Cloud project

  1. Go to console.cloud.google.com/projectcreate.
  2. Project name: BOS Assistant (anything is fine). Click Create.
  3. Wait ~20 seconds, then make sure that project is selected in the top bar.
2

Turn on the APIs you'll use

  1. Open APIs & Services → Library.
  2. Search and Enable each of these: Gmail API, Google Calendar API, Google Tasks API, Google Drive API.
3

Set up the consent screen

  1. Open APIs & Services → OAuth consent screen.
  2. Choose External → Create.
  3. App name: BOS Assistant. User support email: your email. Developer contact: your email. Save and continue.
  4. On Scopes, you can skip adding scopes here (BOS requests them at connect time). Save and continue.
  5. On Test users, click Add Users and add the Google email you'll connect. Save and continue.
Leaving the app in Testing mode is fine and is the fastest path. Any email you add as a Test User can connect immediately. You can add up to 100.
4

Create the OAuth credentials

  1. Open APIs & Services → Credentials.
  2. Click Create Credentials → OAuth client ID.
  3. Application type: Web application. Name: BOS.
  4. Under Authorized redirect URIs, click Add URI and paste this exact value (this is YOUR dashboard's address):
https://YOUR-DASHBOARD-DOMAIN/api/connectors/oauth/google/callback
The redirect URI must match exactly — including https:// and no trailing slash. If it's wrong, Google shows Error 400: redirect_uri_mismatch when you try to connect. The Connectors page in BOS shows your exact URI — copy it from there if unsure.
  1. Click Create. Google shows your Client ID and Client secret. Keep this dialog open.
5

Paste the two values into BOS

  1. In BOS, go to Connectors → Set up your own Google app.
  2. Paste the Client ID and Client secret from Google. Click Save.
  3. Click Connect Google Workspace. Sign in with the Google account you added as a Test User and approve.
If you see an "unverified app" warning, that's expected for a Testing-mode app you just made. Click Advanced → Go to BOS Assistant (unsafe) — it's your own app, it's safe — and continue.

That's it

Your Email & Calendar agent can now read your inbox, draft replies, and manage your calendar. To connect a different Google account later, add it as a Test User in step 3 and connect again.

Trouble?
  • redirect_uri_mismatch → the URI in step 4 doesn't match. Recopy it from the Connectors page.
  • access_denied / not a test user → add that exact Google email under Test Users (step 3).
  • Nothing happens on Connect → make sure you clicked Save after pasting the Client ID/secret.
