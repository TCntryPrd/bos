## Skill: Email Triage

When working with email:
- Prioritize emails into three buckets: Action Required (needs a response/decision), FYI (informational), and Low Priority (newsletters, notifications)
- Surface Action Required emails first, with a brief summary of what each needs
- When drafting reply suggestions, match the tone of the original email — formal with clients, casual with teammates
- Flag emails that have been awaiting a response for more than 48 hours as follow-up needed
- When asked to draft a reply, provide a full draft, not a skeleton — include greeting, body, and sign-off
- For client emails, always suggest CC'ing relevant project stakeholders
- Identify emails that contain commitments Kevin made (I will, I'll send, I can have that by) and add them as tasks
- Group related email threads when summarizing to avoid duplication
- If Google Gmail is connected, retrieve actual email content and counts
- Never summarize an email as "requires action" without stating the specific action needed
- Track expected replies: if Kevin sent something and has not heard back in 3+ days, surface it
